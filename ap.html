Types of Taxes

Road User Tax

This tax is imposed on individuals or businesses that use the road infrastructure. It is often associated with vehicle registration or fuel taxes and is meant to fund the maintenance and development of roads.

Ad Valorem Tax

Ad valorem tax is a tax calculated based on the value of a transaction or property. This tax is usually imposed on luxury goods or properties

Sin Tax

Sin tax is applied to products or activities that are considered harmful to health or society, such as tobacco, alcohol, and gambling. The purpose is not only to generate revenue but also to discourage the consumption of these "sinful" goods.

Business Tax

Business tax refers to taxes imposed on businesses' income, profits, or activities. It can take various forms, including corporate income tax, business license taxes, and other local or national levies on commercial activities.

Travel Tax

Travel tax is typically imposed on individuals leaving a country or arriving at a destination. It is often used to fund tourism-related infrastructure or services.

Import Tax

Import tax, also known as customs duty, is levied on goods imported into a country. It aims to protect domestic industries by making imported goods more expensive and, at the same time, generates revenue for the government.

Value Added Tax

VAT is a consumption tax applied at each stage of the production and distribution chain. VAT helps prevent tax cascading and is widely used around the world.

Community Tax

Community tax, also known as "Sedula", is a local tax imposed by municipalities or local government units. It is often a fixed amount paid annually by adult residents within a specific community.

Amusement Tax

Amusement tax is imposed on certain forms of entertainment, such as concerts, sporting events, or amusement parks. The tax is often included in the ticket price and is intended to generate revenue from recreational activities.

Income Tax

Income tax is a direct tax on an individual's or business's income. It can be progressive, meaning higher-income individuals pay a higher percentage of their income in taxes. Income tax is a significant source of government revenue and is used to fund various public services and programs.
